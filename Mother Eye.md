Mother Eye was a late addition to the story to give it more of a sci-fi feel and to explain how Prevo can control the populace despite being a real jerk of a dictator. I realized late that she could probably be interpreted as a cheap imitation of Big Brother, but I think the differentiators are a lot more terrifying, frankly.

First of all, Mother Eye isn't merely tailoring information. She’s creating a marketplace for emotional comfort. In the market in Chapter 4, Clay sees a merchant selling “Truth Updates.” Which I introduce early on to change the entire moral equation. Without Truth Updates, Mother Eye is a paternalistic information filter. With Truth Updates, the citizens become complicit. “Are you feeling anxious about yesterday’s news? Let me give you an alternate version that doesn’t contradict reality, but will reframe your perception to something easier for you.”

So, it’s not censorship so much as therapeutic reality. You think this is far fetched? This is how Twitter works. This is how people actually behave. Most social media algorithms don't actually convince people of things. They reinforce trajectories already present. If someone is anxious, they get anxiety content. If someone is angry, they get outrage content. If someone is politically committed, they get political validation. The algorithm isn't usually changing beliefs. It's feeding them. Mother Eye is essentially the benevolent version of that mechanism. She isn't telling citizens what to think. She's discovering what they already want to think and gently supplying supporting evidence.

Your typical dystopia is about a government forcing citizens to believe a lie. I’m showing you a dystopia where citizens pay for lies to believe. The specific example from Chapter 4 is, Clay witnessed a bombing at the starport that killed civilians. He knows it’s Klein creating a diversion so he can escape the Tokusha dragnet. Mother Eye can’t have people believing a terrorist escaped capture, so she fabricates a failed terrorist attack in which casualties remain unconfirmed. One of the truth updates that Clay was offered suggested that it was merely an industrial accident, and no injuries were reported. 

So lies are never spoken. Truth is consumed subjectively to your tastes. Nothing contradicts. Much more difficult to propaganda. Clay can’t prove any of those accounts were wrong because nothing contradicts what he saw, only what he believes, and people will generally accept whatever truth fits their belief.

In reality, people experiences discomfort with the world and immediately seeks a service that removes it --- practically a theological argument. Mother Eye offers a version of reality that allows you to remain unchanged. Viewed that way, the Truth Update isn't just worldbuilding. It's a symbolic representation of the antagonist's philosophy. 

Big Brother is about control, surveillance, and thought control. Mother Eye is about reassurance and complacency over truth. The terrifying possibility is that Mother Eye may genuinely create a happier society. The reader can't simply dismiss her as evil. They have to wrestle with the possibility that some people’s lives may be better if they just bought an truth update and went home believing everything was fine (or at least not worth fighting over) instead of joining a rebellion and getting killed.

So, is Mother Eye lying? How much truth does a person need? Is there a point where comfort becomes a form of surrender?
